================================================================================
WHAT SICPA ALREADY HAS (AND THEREFORE WHAT TO AVOID)
================================================================================

SICPA has: SICPATRACE® Evo (backend), regional consumer apps (DGDA Connect,
GRS-SICPA Connect, CalOrigin, Hakiki), Digital Trust Suite (digital identity).

Their apps work. Their problem is they are government-mandated, single-category,
country-specific. Nobody installs them voluntarily. They generate zero collective
intelligence — each scan is verified and discarded, no trust network forms.

The UX layer is their blind spot. That is our angle.


================================================================================
THE CORE INSIGHT
================================================================================

SICPA has the backend. They don't have the front door.

More precisely: they have many front doors (one per country, one per product
category, all ugly). They have no universal, desirable, consumer-grade front
door. And they have no trust aggregation — no network that turns individual
scans into collective intelligence.

We are not competing with their scanning technology.
We are building the layer on top of it that makes it matter at population scale.

One-line pitch: "You built the vault. We built the bank."


================================================================================
CHOSEN STRATEGY: ANGLE 1 + ANGLE 2 ROADMAP SLIDE
================================================================================

PRIMARY DEMO — ANGLE 1: "The UX They Never Built"

  Build the consumer scanning experience for the persona SICPA has never
  designed for: a young adult who scans voluntarily, for their own reasons.

  What makes this different from SICPA's existing apps:
    a) Guided capture: haptic + visual feedback that solves the CDP precision
       problem without the user understanding what CDP is.
    b) Instant, satisfying result: not a government compliance page — a clear,
       emotional, designed outcome screen.
    c) Scan contributes to something: the result feeds a trust score for
       the store the user is standing in. Their scan matters beyond themselves.

  This is the hackathon deliverable. See "What to Build" below.

ROADMAP SLIDE — ANGLE 2: "The Network They're Missing"

  One slide in the pitch deck shows the trust aggregation layer:
    - Individual scans → anonymized → Store Trust Score
    - Passive trust for elderly / non-scanning users via Store Discovery
    - Counterfeit geography map for regulators and brands

  Purpose: show the jury and SICPA that the scanned data has a second life.
  This is not demoed, only shown conceptually.


================================================================================
WHAT TO BUILD ON APRIL 22
================================================================================

Single user journey: Young adult, standing in a pharmacy or grocery store,
scanning a product they're about to buy.

The flow (5 screens):

  1. HOME / TRIGGER
     Clean single-action screen. One button: "Scan a Product."
     No account required to start. No onboarding wall.

  2. GUIDED CAPTURE
     Camera activates. The key UX innovation happens here:
       - Visual frame guide: aligns user to correct distance/angle.
       - Haptic feedback: increasing "tick" frequency as capture quality
         improves (Geiger counter metaphor). No text instructions needed.
       - Auto-trigger: scan fires automatically when quality threshold met.
         No shutter button. User just holds steady.
     This screen is the entire thesis of the demo. Make it feel like magic.

  3. PROCESSING (1–2 seconds)
     Minimal animation. "Reading security signature..."
     Mock the SICPA API response here.

  4. RESULT SCREEN
     Two states:
       AUTHENTIC: Large green signal. Product name + brand.
                  "Scanned by 12 people here today." (trust network seed)
       SUSPICIOUS/FAKE: Clear warning. Report button. Location tag.
     No legal text. No government compliance language. Designed for humans.

  5. CONTRIBUTION CONFIRMATION (optional, 2 seconds)
     "Your scan contributed to this store's trust score: 94%."
     One tap to see the store's full rating. One tap to close.
     Seeds the idea of the network without requiring the user to do anything.

Mock API contract (define this so the demo is stable):
  Input:  { image: base64, location: { lat, lng }, product_hint: string }
  Output: { status: "authentic" | "suspicious" | "fake",
            confidence: 0.0–1.0,
            product: { name, brand, batch, manufactured_at },
            scan_count_here_today: int }

  "1 in 10 medicines in developing markets is fake. SICPA has the technology
  to verify every single one of them. The technology works. The problem is

  [show screenshot of DGDA Connect or GRS-SICPA Connect if permitted]

  [show the single roadmap slide: trust aggregation diagram]

================================================================================
WHAT TO SAY IF ASKED ABOUT THE ELDERLY
================================================================================

"We deliberately chose not to force elderly users to scan. That's a design
trap — it produces a bad experience for everyone. Instead, we designed the
network so that younger users scanning for their own reasons passively
generate trust data that elderly users consume without any action. The
generational trust relay is social infrastructure, not a feature. We're
making explicit what families already do informally."


================================================================================
WHAT TO SAY IF ASKED "WHY NOT NFC?"
================================================================================

"We agree NFC would simplify the capture flow significantly. Our prototype
targets optical CDP because that's what's on products today. NFC is the right
long-term direction as hardware costs fall — and our UX layer is agnostic to
the underlying verification method. If SICPA ships NFC codes tomorrow, this
app works with them. We plug into their backend, whatever form it takes."


================================================================================
CONTINGENCY: IF SICPA CDP SCANNING IS TOO COMPLEX TO DEMO RELIABLY
================================================================================

If on the morning of April 22 the CDP capture proves too unreliable to demo
cleanly (bad lighting, too many failures), pivot immediately:

  Drop the guided capture screen. Replace it with a simple QR scan.
  The innovation narrative shifts to: "We solved the trust aggregation layer
  that SICPA's apps are missing. The capture tech is a placeholder for their
  backend — what we're showing is the network intelligence on top."

  This is Option D from the strategic analysis: "Zero Scan, Full Network."
  Weaker demo, but still a coherent and differentiated pitch.


================================================================================
WHAT NOT TO BUILD
================================================================================

  - Gamification (Trust Credits, leaderboards) — optional overlay, cut first
  - Whistleblower / fake reporting flow — mention in pitch, don't build
  - Store owner dashboard — mention as roadmap, don't build
  - Manufacturer analytics portal — architecture only, don't build
  - Account creation / onboarding — user goes straight to scan
  - Any explanation of what CDP is — the UX abstracts it completely
